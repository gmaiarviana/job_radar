Career Narrative — Guilherme Maia
Purpose: Source-of-truth document that defines who Guilherme is professionally — purpose, values, direction, experience, and evidence base. Feeds all derived materials (resumes, cover letters, LinkedIn, applications). Language: English (base). Status: Draft v2 — Feb 2026.

1. Purpose
Guilherme investigates why things are the way they are. Before solving a problem, he questions the premises: why is this a problem, why now, what changed, what beliefs sustain the current behavior. This posture — which he recognizes as philosophical — is not a parallel interest to his career. It is the engine of it.
The core conviction: clarity of thought precedes behavioral change. Beliefs generate behaviors, behaviors generate routines, routines generate results. When an organization or team is underperforming, the root cause is almost always poorly examined premises — ill-defined scope, ambiguous responsibilities, misaligned priorities. Guilherme finds fulfillment when he brings that clarity — whether reorganizing teams, refining product requirements, or structuring how humans and AI agents work together.
Human communication is the thread that runs through everything. Guilherme understands that language is the species' greatest tool for collaboration — and at the same time, it is profoundly limited. There are multiple layers between the speaker's intention and the listener's interpretation. Reducing that noise — being precise, eliminating ambiguity, structuring what is disorganized — is what he does in every role he holds. It is also what draws him to philosophy of language and to questions about how meaning is actually communicated — beyond the words used to express it.
Information technology is the chosen channel because it is where human communication and collaboration are being most deeply transformed at this moment in history. Guilherme has no attachment to the industry itself — his attachment is to the type of impact. If tomorrow a different channel becomes more relevant, he will migrate. As he already did from construction to tech.
2. Values
Simplicity. Because unnecessary complexity is noise. Noise prevents clarity. Clarity is a prerequisite for good decisions. Guilherme seeks the simplest solution that solves the problem — in code, in processes, in communication.
Transparency. Because opaque decisions breed distrust, and distrust destroys collaboration. Guilherme exposes risks, limitations, and uncertainties openly. He prefers saying "I don't know" over building a fragile narrative.
Collaboration. Because no individual has every angle. Guilherme believes that the convergence of diverse perspectives — when organized with clarity — produces results no one could achieve alone. This is why he values environments with autonomy and diversity of thought.
Traits that appear consistently throughout the trajectory and sustain these values:
Initiative. Recurring pattern across the entire career: identifies opportunities and acts before receiving a mandate. PO guild, innovation pipeline, GenAI PoCs, Area 51, R&D — all were voluntary initiatives.
Continuous learning. The career migration is the largest example, but the pattern repeats at smaller scales: each phase brings a new technology or domain that Guilherme absorbs through necessity and curiosity. From Spark/Scala to LangGraph, from construction site management to SAFe PI Planning.
Intentionality. Career decisions are deliberate. Every role change had an articulated reason. Guilherme is not reactive — he chooses a direction and then seeks or creates the conditions to follow it.
3. Career Vision
Guilherme wants to be in positions where reflecting, organizing complex thought, and bringing clarity are recognized as valuable contributions — not as luxury or secondary activity.
In the short and medium term, this translates to: consolidating as a manager who is called for strategic problems. Someone above senior level — not by title, but by type of demand. The person who enters when the problem is ambiguous, the scope is uncertain, and nobody knows where to start.
He sees himself as a generalist manager: capable of operating end-to-end across product, program, or project, adapting to what the context requires. His career confirms this — from construction engineer to PO, TPM, and PM, each transition was deliberate. The market asks for specialization, and Guilherme understands this. But his value proposition is precisely the ability to move between roles while maintaining a consistent lens: clarity, structure, evidence.
Areas of interest for impact: food, environment, energy, education, mental health. The specific sector matters less than two conditions: (1) the problem must have direct impact on society, and (2) the environment must value autonomy and innovation.
In the long term, Guilherme wants to build the reputation that gives him access to decisions at scale. The possible paths — entrepreneurship, academic research, executive leadership, content production, community leadership — are all under simultaneous construction at different stages. The strategy is to accumulate concrete results, network, and knowledge that allow any of these paths to become viable when the moment arrives.
His AI work methodology is part of this construction. Through iterative experimentation across multiple personal and professional projects, Guilherme developed a systematic approach to human-AI collaboration — documented in Pillar 4. The patterns are practical and evidence-based, not theoretical.
He believes there is room for fundamentally more efficient approaches, and this conviction drives an emerging interest in academic research at the intersection of NLP, knowledge representation, and computational efficiency. This is not yet a formal research program — it is a direction he is actively exploring and preparing for.
A longer-term interest connects to this: Guilherme sees significant opportunity in rethinking how current AI architectures work. Today's systems rely on billions of parameters, training on the entire internet's data, and massive computational and energy costs — often with results that don't justify the cost. He believes there is room for fundamentally more efficient approaches. This conviction drives an emerging interest in academic research. Pillar 4: AI as a Tool and as a Work Methodology

Two dimensions. The first:
identifies opportunities for AI application in existing products and processes,
conducts PoCs from problem identification to architecture design to implementation,
builds working prototypes using LLM APIs, RAG patterns, and agent frameworks.
Prompt engineering with systematic testing (test-driven prompt optimization).

Uses AI agents and tools for coding, automation, and experimentation
The second — and more distinctive — is a methodology for integrating AI into workflows, developed through iterative experimentation across multiple projects. Four patterns emerged:
(1) Documentation as infrastructure for AI collaboration. AI agents are only as useful as the context they receive. Without clear, navigable structure, hallucination increases and every new chat session requires re-explaining intentions. Guilherme structures projects with living documentation — architecture decisions, component responsibilities, roadmaps where the next priority is refined and the rest stays high-level. No over-planning; focus on the current pain. The architecture itself is componentized and decoupled so that changes don't require touching multiple components simultaneously. The evolution was concrete: early on, each session started with lengthy context-setting. Now, the documentation is organized so that agents read the overviews and operate with minimal re-explanation.
(2) Explicit human-AI role governance. Clarity about what is expected from each actor — human or AI — prevents conflicts of responsibility and extracts the best from each stage. In practice: one tool refines and evaluates, another implements, another updates documentation. The separation is deliberate — it mirrors how effective human teams work. Each component has a defined scope, and the flow operates without ambiguity about who does what. This also enables quality checkpoints: one AI reviews and evaluates the work of another, just as a senior engineer would review a junior's code.
(3) Criteria-based evaluation systems. LLM outputs are non-deterministic by default — each run can produce a different result, which makes raw output unreliable. But when explicit criteria and expectations are established, the responsibility shifts: the task is no longer "produce an evaluation" but "fulfill the evaluation criteria." This principle applies equally to human and institutional systems — evaluation should never depend solely on individual judgment without shared standards. Guilherme builds evaluation infrastructure (rubrics, gabaritos, LLM-as-a-judge, multi-model comparison) so that quality measurement is systematic, learnings accumulate, and criteria expand collaboratively over time.
(4) Decomposition into specialized pipelines. A single large prompt carries too much responsibility, too much context, and too little focus. Decomposing into specialized stages — each with a narrow scope and specific prompt — increases quality, reduces hallucination, and lightens context requirements. The design challenge — and a space Guilherme wants to explore further — is mapping the flow of thought: the path through which an idea is processed, stage by stage. The more precise the description of each stage, the more efficient the system becomes.
Honest boundary: Applies AI capabilities to solve business problems and builds functional prototypes. Articulates what and why of AI components (RAG, agents, multi-agent systems). Technical depth on intermediate architecture details (chunking strategies, embedding models, reranking, graph state management) is developing — coordinates with engineers for production-grade design decisions.
